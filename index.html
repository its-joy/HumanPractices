537 million adults are living with diabetes

Diabetes is one of the most prevalent chronic diseases of the twenty-first century

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the global prevalence of diabetes has nearly doubled since 1980, with numbers skyrocketing to an estimated 463 million adults living with diabetes worldwide in 2021. This number is expected to rise to 578 million by 2030 and 700 million by 2045.

In 2021, diabetes was responsible for an estimated 4.2 million deaths worldwide

It is one of the leading causes of premature death and disability worldwide

High blood sugar levels over time can damage blood vessels, nerves, and organs, leading to a range of health problems such as heart disease, stroke, kidney disease, blindness, and amputations.

Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGMs) are amongst the most popular diabetes self management tool- however, they have many limitations that hinder their accessibility

Lack of access to self-management tools can lead to poor diabetes control, increased risk of complications, and higher healthcare costs.
