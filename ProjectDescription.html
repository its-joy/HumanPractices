Microneedle Coating and Testing

Microneedles offer a solution for diabetes patients seeking a minimally invasive and more effective alternative to conventional needles used in Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGMs). While traditional needles may cause minimal pain, microneedle arrays have the potential to revolutionize glucose sensing by improving the lifespan of glucose-sensing enzymes and enhancing the sensitivity of glucose sensors. The high surface area of microneedle arrays provides ample immobilization sites for enzymes, which increases enzyme loading capacity and stability, thereby protecting the immobilized enzyme and enhancing sensitivity to glucose fluctuation in Interstitial Fluid (ISF). Moreover, when combined with a machine learning model and algorithm, microneedle arrays can provide more precise and accurate glucose readings, further enhancing their effectiveness. The combination of microneedle arrays and machine learning has the potential to increase the integrability of CGMs with other diabetes management tools, closing the loop and offering a more comprehensive and effective personal health management solution.

The combination of MAPs and microneedles presents an exciting avenue for enhancing the stability and lifespan of biosensors. With its high concentration of DOPA residue, MAPs offer a unique opportunity to form an even film on the surface of electrodes through electrochemical oxidation in one step.

The process begins with the preparation of a MAP and GOx mixture in acetate buffer. The mixture is then subjected to electropolymerization on the working electrode of the microneedle sensor, utilizing potential cycling from -0.3-0.7 V against Ag/AgCl at a scan rate of 100mVs-1. During this process, the oxidation of MAP molecules and their covalent bonding with GOx enzymes occur simultaneously in one step, resulting in the formation of an even layer on the microneedle's surface. The electrochemical oxidation method for coating microneedles with MAPs and GOx offers numerous benefits, including improved stability, extended lifespan, and enhanced sensitivity of glucose sensors. Our approach is a promising step forward in the development of more robust biosensors that can deliver accurate, reliable glucose readings for diabetes management.
